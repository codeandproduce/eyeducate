Contact Lenses vs Glasses / Chinese Title

By: Rachel Hsu

More than six in ten people wear either glasses, contact lenses, or some form of vision correction. This means that the majority of the world’s population have had to make a choice between contacts lenses or glasses. This article addresses the benefits and drawback of both forms of eyesight support. Hopefully, you will find this useful.

Glasses: Benefits
Most of the population choose to wear glasses due to general convenience, it is more accessible and anyone, regardless of age, race, gender, or eye shape can wear them. Glasses also lower the risk of eye infection or even just irritating the eyes in general. From an economic standpoint, glasses are also more affordable and they do not require the user to change them often.

Glasses: Drawbacks
The main concerns about glasses is their constant weight and presence when wearing them. It can provide inconvenient to some, especially when participating in athletic activities, they can easily fall and break. Glasses also tend to fog up when temperatures change, and rain and snow will also obstruct the lenses.

Contact Lenses: Benefits
Because the lenses sit directly on the eyes, there is next to no vision distortion and the scope of vision is wider than glasses. Contacts also provide no trouble when engaging in exercise or sports, they also are unaffected by weather and temperature conditions. And finally, contact lenses are often chosen for aesthetic purposes, as some believe the addition of glasses is more unattractive than a plain face.

Contact Lenses: Drawbacks
These lenses take some time to adjust to, and they can be difficult to apply properly. Moreover, direct contact with the eyes increase the chance of eye irritation and infections, this also applies to falling asleep with daily contacts. Speaking of which, daily disposable eye contacts can be expensive long term. The other alternative is monthly or yearly contacts, both which require lens care to avoid infection, which can provide tedious to some.
