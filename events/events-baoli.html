Our trip to 保利叶都 幼儿园

7 January 2019

It was the first day of winter break, and the EYEducate took a journey across Shanghai to teach at a school. The trip included nine club members collaborating to teach seven classes. The lesson circled around the easiest ways to protect your eyes, what harms your eyes, and a video on the importance of taking care of your vision. The kids were introduced to the different types of light and the harm they may bring to their eyes. As one of the underclassmen, I mainly helped as much as possible and watched as an older member: Alex taught the class. I watched as the kids laughed to every small joke we said, and gave us their undivided attention. Since technology dominates what kids view as technology, we taught the harms of technology to eyesight, what not to do, and the students had many queries on the topic. The trip required leadership and the ability to calm kids at a very young age, a quality that all of us, the teachers demonstrated. I saw a smile spread across the room, as if the students were hooked by every word we said. Posture is a big part in protecting vision and health, so the members taught the students how to sit with animals and demonstrations. The hunched back shrimp showed incorrect posture, whereas the beautiful swan showed how you’re supposed to sit. The lesson also included a humorous and entertaining video that the kids definitely enjoyed. At the end of the video, there was a song and dance that all the “teachers” of the day danced to with the kids, jumping up and down, goofing around. Finally, the journey ended with an image of the students and club members holding up a banner with the club motto: “看见世界看见爱”. It was a great day trip, and as a member I’d like to think helped made a positive impact on these student because next time they watch tv they’ll remember the twenty minute rule to protect their eyesight.

EYEducate is a non-profit organization based in Shanghai American School
