ARTICLE I. NAME OF ORGANIZATION

 

The name of the organization is EYEDUCATE.

 

ARTICLE II. PURPOSE

 

Section 1. Nonprofit Purpose

 

This organization is organized solely for charitable purposes, more specifically, to protect the vision of students across China. For this purpose, this corporation/chapter shall teach classes, fundraise funds, and organize trips to various parts of China.

 

Section 2. Specific Purpose

 

EYEducate shall raise funds for educating children in China through various means. With the funds, the organization/chapter may purchase eye-protection kits or sunglasses to be donated to schools and students in need. These trips may also be hosted for the purpose of teaching eye-protection lessons or other activities agreed upon by the schools the corporation/chapter and the students is in.

 

The specific objectives of this organization shall be:

 

•  to teach eye-protection lessons to students across China;

 

•  to provide eye-protection kits to students in rural China as needed;

 

•  to generate funds through bake sales and other activities as see fit by the school the corporation/chapter is located in;

 

•  to communicate with local schools to set up lessons and donations;

 

 

ARTICLE III. MEMBERSHIP

 

Section 1. Eligibility for Membership

 

Membership for any student or teacher in the school the corporation/chapter is located in, supporting the purpose statement in Article II, Section 2, can be granted by the officers or the school. Membership shall only be granted after the proper application process stated by the school.

 

Section 2. Rights of Members

 

Each member may voice his or her opinion during regular meetings as stated in Article IV, section 1, or special meetings provided in Article IV, Section 2. Members may also freely communicate with officers regarding concerns or suggestions. After attaining a year of membership, members may run for an officer position on the through any process approved by the officers and two-thirds (2/3) of the members. Members that were removed shall have to wait for at least half a year until they may run for an officer position again.

 

Section 3. Resignation and Termination

 

Any member may resign by communicating with at least one officer with adequate reasoning. A member may also have their membership terminated by mutual agreement by the officers. Other members may appeal to the officers if two-thirds (2/3) of members request for a member’s membership to be terminated.

 

Section 4. Committees

 

Members are required to join a committee after a initial period of two (2) months, and may only change committees after serving six (6) months in that committee. 

 

ARTICLE IV. MEETINGS OF MEMBERS

 

Section 1. Regular Meetings

 

Regular meetings of the members shall be held weekly, at a time and place designated by the executives or the school that the chapter is located in. At regular meetings the members and executives shall discuss upcoming events, work on projects or activities, and manage any concerns or problems. Members may also break into groups to work on more specific tasks as see fit by the executives. Failure to attend may result in membership termination.

 

Section 2. Special Meetings

 

Special meetings of the members may be called at the request of officers or two-thirds (2/3) of the members at a time and location agreed upon previously. Failure to attend special meetings without adequate reasoning and prior notice will not be punished but noted. The officers should but is not required to attend depending on the situation.

 

Section 3. Notice of Meetings

 

Regular meetings of members shall always be held unless notified otherwise by officers through email or WeChat not less than 24 hours prior to the meeting. Notice of special meetings of members may be in any form including email, verbal, and written notice.

 

Section 4. Quorum

 

A quorum for a regular meeting shall consists of officers and all members with vital roles in recent projects. The quorum for a special meeting shall consists of officers and any necessary member(s).

 

ARTICLE V. EXECUTIVES/OFFICERS

 

Section 1. General Powers

 

The overall direction and the essentials of the corporation shall be managed by its Executives/Officers. The officers shall have control of and be responsible for the management of the affairs and property of the Corporation. With sufficient reasoning and full approval from every member of the officers and two-thirds (2/3) of the members of public, a Director may break, no more than two (2) rules at a time, in this Constitution. Frequent trivial use or abuse of this power may result in removal of a position or even termination of membership.

 

Section 2. Number, Tenure, Requirements, and Qualifications

 

The number of officers shall be decided by any regulations in the school the corporation/chapter is in. The officers shall include the following officers: the President, the Vice-President, the Secretary, the Treasurer, and the Public Relations.

 

Upon election, officers shall immediately enter upon their role and carry out the performance of their duties. They shall stay in office until their successor is duly elected and qualified or if they are removed of position by mutual agreement by officers. All officers must be approved by the other officers. The members of public may also appeal to the officers if they are discontent.

 

Each officer shall only be eligible to be elected if they have been a member of the corporation/chapter for up to one year, unless in the case of a special situation.

 

Each officer shall attend all regular and special meetings unless he/she completed the directions stated in Article IV, Section 4, and Section 5 of this Article.

 

Section 3. Regular Meetings

 

There shall be a regular meeting for officers and any member invited or wish to attend with prior notification weekly, at a time and location designated by the President. At special meetings, the officers (and attending members) shall discuss the essentials of the corporation, and address the current or more distant projects and concerns. They may also work on specific tasks if needed. Failure to attend frequently may result in removal of a position on the officer team and possibly termination of membership.

 

Section 4. Special Meetings

 

Special meetings of officers or any member meeting the requirements stated in Section 3 of this article may be called by a mutual agreement by officers. The time and location must be agreed upon previously. Infrequent failure to attend without adequate reasoning and prior notice will not be punished but will be if frequent, including the removal of a position and possibly termination of membership.

 

Section 5. Notice of Meetings

 

Regular meetings shall always be held unless agreed upon by the officers otherwise. This decision shall not be made unless a quorum of the officers is present as stated in Section 6 of this Article. Notice of special meetings may be in any form including email, verbal, and written notice.

 

Section 6. Quorum

 

A quorum for regular and special meetings shall consists of no less than a third of the current officers, including the officers: the President or the Vice-President, the Secretary, and the Treasurer or the Public Relations. Any members of the public essential to the content of the meeting is also required.

 

Section 7. Forfeiture and Removal

 

Any member officer who fail to fulfill all or any of his or her requirements as set forth in Section 2 of this Article shall forfeit his or her seat through mutual agreement by other officers. Officers who are removed for failure to meet all of any of the requirements of Section 2 of this Article retain the rights of members of public provided in Article III, Section 3, unless terminated of membership by mutual agreement by other officers.

 

Section 8. Vacancies

 

Whenever any vacancy occurs for officer positions, it shall be filled without undue delay by any process determined at a regular or special meeting. Vacancies may be created and filled according to any method approved by the officers as pursuant to Section 1, 3, 4, 7 of this Article.

 

 

ARTICLE VI. OFFICERS

 

The officers shall be the President, the Vice-President, the Secretary, the Treasurer, and Public Relations. All officers must have the status of active members of the Board of Directors. In the case where an officer is extremely busy with work outside of the corporation, he/she may notify the President and under the approval of the other officers arrange for another officer or member to fulfill his/her duty.

 

Section 1. President

 

The President shall oversee the affairs and general direction of the corporation. The President duties shall include:

 

•  He/She shall preside at and may host all meetings of the corporation/chapter.

 

•  He/She shall designate tasks for the other officers and expect, consequently reviewing the progress of the officer.

 

•  He/She shall plan or evaluate ideas from other members for future projects.

 

•  He/She shall organize the structure, schedule, and other aspects of the corporation.

 

•  He/She may enact temporary policies within the corporation as he/she see fit in the best interest of the corporation.

 

Section 2. Vice-President

 

The Vice-President shall be vested with all the powers and shall perform all the duties of the President; fully during the absence the latter, and partially in the presence of the President. The Vice-President’s duty is:

 

•  He/She shall share the burden of the President and aid with communication between the President and the rest of the officer team.

 

Section 3. Secretary

 

The Secretary shall preside all meetings of the corporation and serve as a clerk. The Secretary’s duties shall consists of: 

 

•  He/She shall record the critical decisions in all corporate meetings.

 

•  He/She shall help the President manage or attend meetings within the school the corporation/chapter is in.

 

•  He/She shall be in charge of all notifications of corporate meetings 

 

•  He/She shall communicate with the school the corporation/chapter is in to aid and promote the corporation/chapter.

 

•  He/She shall fill out all the documents and information necessary for the corporation.

 

•  He/She shall keep track of the attendance for regular meetings of members.

 

Section 4. Treasurer

 

The Treasurer shall keep track of all financial flow and organize funds in the best interest of the corporation. The Treasurer’s duties shall be:

 

•  He/She shall keep a record of all transaction of funds of the corporation.

 

•  He/She shall provide funds for members when necessary, such as before bake sales, and request the funds back along with the profit afterwards.

 

•  He/She plan fundraising activities.

 

•  He/She shall purchase necessary commodities.

 

Section 5. Public Relations

 

The Public Relations shall care for the public image of the corporation and communicate with parties outside of the corporation or school the corporation/chapter is in. His/Her duties shall include:

 

•  He/She shall manage and update the corporation’s social media accounts and website.

 

•  He/She shall work with the Secretary to:

 

•                    communicate with parties outside of the corporation, such as local schools, vendors, and other corporations/organizations/chapters.

 

•                    He/She shall corporate with the Secretary in advertising the corporation/chapter within and outside the school he/she is in

 

•  He/She shall purchase necessary equipment from vendors.

 

•  He/She shall communicate with other chapters.

 

 

Section 6. Election of Officers

 

Officers may be elected pursuant to Article III, Section 2.

 

 

ARTICLE VII. AMENDMENTS

 

The Articles and Bylaws may be amended in any manner at any regular or special meeting by the officers, but the suggestion must be made one meeting prior to the decision-making meeting. No amendment shall be made unless the quorum stated in Article V, Section 6 is fulfilled, and shall require the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of all members of the corporation. Any amendment must be announced seven (7) days before enacted verbally and through email.
